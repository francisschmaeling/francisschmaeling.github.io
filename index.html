Teaching Strategies for ELAR / ESL

Here are three core strategies I implement to ensure student success in my ELAR and ESL classrooms.

1. Scaffolding with Graphic Organizers

Context: ELAR & ESL Instruction

I utilize graphic organizers (such as Venn diagrams, concept maps, and story boards) to break down complex texts. This scaffolds the learning process, allowing students to visually organize their thoughts before moving to written composition. This is particularly effective for English Language Learners (ELLs) to lower the cognitive load while analyzing literature.

2. Think-Pair-Share (Collaborative Learning)

Context: Encouraging Speaking & Listening

To foster a welcoming and inclusive classroom, I use Think-Pair-Share. Students first process a question individually (Think), discuss it with a peer (Pair), and then share with the class (Share). This reduces anxiety for ELLs and ensures 100% participation, maximizing verbal practice.

3. Total Physical Response (TPR) & Contextual Vocabulary

Context: Vocabulary Acquisition

Drawing from my time at the Richardson Adult Literacy Center, I use Total Physical Response (TPR) to connect vocabulary with physical movements. For secondary ELAR students, I adapt this by using "Concept Charades" or acting out scenes from literature. This multi-sensory approach helps solidify definitions and concepts for students from diverse linguistic backgrounds.
